Vote Smart, Stay Informed

Discover your rights and responsibilities as a voter in India.

Constitutional Voting Rights

Every Indian citizen aged 18 and above has the right to vote as per Article 326 of the Indian Constitution.

  • Universal Adult Franchise: All citizens above 18 years are entitled to vote.
  • Equal Voting Rights: Every vote has equal weightage regardless of caste, creed, or gender.
  • Right to Participate in Democracy: Voting is an essential right that allows citizens to choose their government.

The Voting Process

Understand the steps involved in voting and making your voice heard in a democratic system.

  1. Register to vote through the Election Commission of India.
  2. Receive your voter ID card.
  3. Go to your assigned polling station on Election Day.
  4. Present your ID and cast your vote on the EVM (Electronic Voting Machine).

Why Vote?

Voting is not just a right but a responsibility. Here's why your vote matters:

  • Shape the Future: Your vote helps elect leaders who will shape policies and laws affecting your future.
  • Be a Part of the Process: Voting ensures that your voice is heard in the democratic process.
  • Protect Your Rights: Voting ensures that your rights and interests are protected and represented.
