Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Who is eligible to vote in India?

Any Indian citizen who is 18 years of age or older is eligible to vote, provided they are registered in the electoral roll of their constituency.

2. How do I register to vote?

To register, visit the official website of the Election Commission of India or your local electoral office and fill out the necessary forms with valid proof of identity and address.

3. What are the key documents required for voter registration?

You need proof of Indian citizenship, proof of age (18 years or above), and proof of residence (e.g., a utility bill, passport, etc.).

4. Is voting compulsory in India?

No, voting is not compulsory in India. However, it is a constitutional right, and citizens are encouraged to vote to participate in the democratic process.

5. How can I check if I am registered to vote?

You can check your voter registration status online on the official website of the Election Commission of India or by contacting the electoral office in your area.
